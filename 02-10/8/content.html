Random Tickets

One day, I was scheduled to fly somewhere, but I lost my plane tickets! Luckily for me, I remember that I was traveling from airport 1 to airport N; however, I do not remember where I was supposed to transfer flights (if at all). To solve this problem, I decide to travel from airport 1 to airport N with the following process. At airport u, I choose a ticket uniformly at random (which must originate at airport u) and use it. This puts me at airport v. If I'm not at airport N, then I repeat this process until I reach airport N. Could you please compute the expected number of flights my trip will entail to the nearest integer?

Input

The first line contains an integer T (the number of test cases). T test cases follow.

The first line of each test case contains integers N and M. N represents the number of airports on my map, and M represents the number of tickets I could possibly use. M lines follow as described.

On each of the subsequent M lines are two integers u, v. Each line indicates that there exists a ticket from airport u to airport v. Each unordered pair (u, v) is guaranteed to be listed at most once.

The provided graph is guaranteed to be a directed acyclic graph (in particular, there are no cycles). Also, for each ticket t, there is guaranteed to exist a path P from 1 to N such that P includes t.

Output

Output T real numbers, one on each line. The ith number should represent the ith answer rounded to three digits after the decimal point. For example, 0.7555 rounds to 0.756, 100.10101 rounds to 100.101, and 5 would be printed as 5.000.

Constraints

1 <= T <= 10
2 <= N, M <= 100000
1 <= u, v <= N

Sample Input

2
5 6
1 3
1 2
3 4
2 4
3 5
4 5
2 1
1 2

Sample Output

2.750
1.000

Explanation

In the first case, I have two tickets I can take from airport 1. If I fly to airport 3, then I will have two more options: 4 and 5. If I take 5, then I'm at my destination, and if I take 4, then I'll have to take a ticket to 5 for my next flight. On the other hand, if I go to 2 first, then I will definitely fly to 4 and then to 5. Taking the expected value over all of these choices, I will reach airport 5 in 2.75 flights on average.

In the second case, I can only go straight to my final destination.
